Stay Safe Online

Cyber Awareness Center

Learn how to protect yourself from cyber crimes

Financial Fraud

  • Never share OTP, CVV, or PIN with anyone — banks never ask for these
  • Verify UPI payment requests before accepting
  • Use official banking apps only — avoid third-party links
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all financial accounts
  • Report unauthorized transactions within 24 hours to your bank

Cyber Bullying

  • Screenshot and save all evidence of harassment
  • Block the harasser immediately on all platforms
  • Report to the platform's abuse/safety team
  • Do not respond to trolls — it encourages them
  • Seek support from trusted adults or counselors

Hacking

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account
  • Enable 2FA on email, social media, and banking
  • Keep your OS and apps updated
  • Avoid public WiFi for sensitive transactions
  • Use a reputable antivirus and firewall

Social Media Safety

  • Set profiles to private — limit who can see your info
  • Don't accept friend requests from unknown people
  • Never share personal details like address or ID numbers
  • Be cautious of links shared via DMs
  • Report fake profiles impersonating you immediately

Phishing Protection

  • Check the sender's email address carefully for typos
  • Don't click links in suspicious emails or SMS
  • Verify website URLs — look for HTTPS and correct domain
  • Banks and government agencies never ask for passwords via email
  • When in doubt, contact the organization directly

Child Safety Online

  • Monitor children's online activities and set parental controls
  • Teach children not to share personal info online
  • Be aware of signs of online grooming
  • Report any CSAM content immediately to cybertipline
  • Encourage open communication about online experiences
